The Anthropic dimension

This flywheel has an external loop Jordan's LinkedIn journey doesn't: Anthropic itself. Every time Marcus posts about Claude 101 and tags @Anthropic, he creates a signal Anthropic's comms and policy teams can act on. This is a deliberate, documentable public narrative that SoG controls.

Marcus's posting plan — AI-led weighting, LinkedIn secondary

Weighting note: Marcus's primary audience is the AI/tech sector, youth employment advocates, and Anthropic directly. Posts marked AI lead are the priority. Posts marked LinkedIn serve a broader professional network.

Getting Anthropic to notice — and ideally respond

Why this is a credible ask
  • SoG is a registered charity working with 15–21 year olds facing genuine social exclusion
  • Marcus co-designed the curriculum — this is youth-led, not top-down
  • The certificate is a concrete, verifiable artefact Anthropic can point to
  • SoG's use case aligns with Anthropic's stated mission — AI that benefits everyone
  • The programme is scalable nationally via AIM+ and Phase 3 equivalents
What we are actually asking for
  • A public acknowledgement — a comment, repost, or mention — that legitimises the certificate
  • Ideally: Anthropic co-brands the certificate so it carries weight on a CV
  • Optionally: SoG included as a case study in Anthropic's social impact reporting
  • Not asking for funding initially — asking for recognition, which costs nothing

Outreach sequence

Step 1Marcus posts his certificate on LinkedIn
Tags @Anthropic directly. Frames it as "the first young person from a SoG cohort to complete Claude 101." Personal and specific — not promotional. SoG staff engage within the hour.
Step 2SoG reshares with organisational framing
SoG's page shares Marcus's post with a brief statement about the co-designed programme. Tags Anthropic's company page. Links to any published write-up.
Step 3Direct outreach to Anthropic comms
Sohail emails Anthropic's communications team directly. Subject: "Young person co-designs AI literacy programme with SoG — certificate issued." Attaches the post link, programme summary, and certificate. Asks specifically for a comment or repost.
Step 4Sector press creates external signal
Third Sector or Civil Society News runs a short piece. This moves Anthropic from "optional engagement" to "it would look odd not to respond." Marcus's story is the hook; programme scalability is the angle.

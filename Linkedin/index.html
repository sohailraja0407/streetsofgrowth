    Jordan's journey — Phase 3 · 6-month post plan

    Practical posting guidance for Jordan and SoG staff

    What to post
    • Honest reflections — what surprised you, what was hard
    • Specific skills from work experience sessions
    • A question your mentor helped you answer
    • Something a peer taught you
    • Before/after: who you were vs who you're becoming
    • A photo from a placement or SoG event (with consent)
    What to avoid
    • Vague or generic updates ("Had a great week!")
    • Sharing anything personal about other participants
    • Complaining about specific employers or placements
    • Posting when emotionally reactive — write it, wait, then post
    • Copying others' posts — your voice is the value
    • Oversharing — professional doesn't mean emotionless
    How SoG staff support
    • Review draft posts before publishing if Jordan wants
    • Like and comment within 24 hours of posting
    • Tag relevant employer partners where appropriate
    • Reshare to SoG's organisational page
    • Screenshot notable engagement to celebrate in sessions
    • Suggest post ideas based on session content each week
    Profile basics first
    • Professional or clear headshot — no group photos
    • Headline: "Phase 3 participant @ Streets of Growth | [sector interest]"
    • About section: 3–4 sentences, first-person, honest about the journey
    • Add Streets of Growth as experience (start date = Phase 3 start)
    • List any work experience placements as experience entries
    • Connect with SoG staff, partners, and work experience supervisors

    On consent and safeguarding

    All posts should be written and controlled by the young person. SoG staff should not post on their behalf without explicit consent. Any tagging of SoG accounts or employer partners should be agreed in advance. Young people should be made aware that LinkedIn is a public platform and that posts may be seen by future employers, funders, and others beyond their current network.

Understanding the Indices

Harm Reduction Index
Safety YP (35%) + Safety IC (40%) + Inverted Risk (25%)
How far the young person has moved away from harm and unsafe situations. A rising score means they are safer (both self-reported and coach-assessed) and presenting lower risk to self and others.
EET Readiness Index
Emotional Regulation (50%) + Self Efficacy (50%)
How ready the young person is to enter and sustain education, employment or training. A rising score shows growing emotional stability and self-belief — the foundations needed to thrive in EET.
Composite Journey Index
Harm Reduction (50%) + EET Readiness (50%)
The headline score summarising overall progress. Becoming safer AND becoming more ready for a positive future. This is the single number that tracks holistic change.

Key Milestones — What Happened and Why

The Story This Tells

Why the Relapse Matters Most

The journey chart shows a young person's Composite Journey Index starting at 31/100 and rising steadily through Phase 2 engagement. By month 12, the score reaches 71.2 — the strongest point yet. Risk is at 4, Safety at 78, and all indicators are positive.

Then comes the relapse. Note: in this model, the relapse has been accentuated for demonstration purposes — a real relapse episode would typically produce a smaller, shorter dip before the structured programme response takes effect. Here, at month 13, a concerning incident causes Risk to spike back to 20, Safety to collapse from 78 to 44, and the Composite Index to drop to 24. This is the most important moment on the chart — not because it represents failure, but because of what happens next.

The programme's structured crisis response activates: intensive 1:1 harm reduction support, Wheel of Life and Vision/Reality tools, Stages of Change assessment. Phase 2 is extended by 3 months. By month 15 the young person has recovered to a Composite of 55.8, and the upward trajectory resumes.

This pattern — progress, setback, structured response, recovery — is the programme working as intended. It demonstrates that AIM+ doesn't just support young people in ideal conditions; it has an evidence-based response when things go wrong. The 12-month post-exit follow-up confirms the outcome is sustained: Risk at 0, Safety at 108, in employment at London Living Wage. All programme outcomes met.

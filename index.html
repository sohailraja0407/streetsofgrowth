These are examples, not case studies. Yasmin and Mohammad are fictional young people created to illustrate what is possible at the Maintain & Exit stage of AIM+. The frameworks are real and ready to use — but the names, posts, and journeys shown are illustrative. Every young person's path through Phase 3 will be different, and these frameworks should be adapted to reflect their own voice, sector interests, and EET goals.

What is a framework — and why does it matter?

A framework is a structured plan that shows not just what to do, but why each step matters and how it connects to the next. These are not scripts. They are starting points that each young person adapts in their own voice, at their own pace, supported by their SoG Intervention Coach and Employability Coach.

They are designed specifically for Phase 3 — the point in the AIM+ journey where a young person is ready to consolidate their progress and move towards independence and a sustained EET outcome.

Each framework is built around a flywheel — a concept from business strategy that describes how small, consistent actions build momentum over time. In plain terms, the flywheel works like this:

The flywheel — how momentum builds

1
A young person takes one action — posts on LinkedIn, earns a certificate, shares what they have learned.
↓
2
SoG staff and partners respond — they comment, share, and amplify. The post reaches employers and funders SoG already knows.
↓
3
Peers see it and feel it is possible — another young person in the cohort thinks "if they can do it, I can." They take their own first step.
↓
4
SoG's reputation grows — authentic youth voices build credibility with funders, commissioners, and new employer partners.
↓
5
The original young person benefits — more connections, more visibility, more evidence of growth to take into job applications. The wheel keeps turning.

The key insight is that no single post or certificate changes everything on its own. What changes things is consistency — small actions, repeated over months, that compound into something employers and funders can see and trust.
